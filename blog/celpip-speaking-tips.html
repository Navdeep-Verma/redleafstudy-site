CELPIP Speaking Tips: All 8 Tasks Explained

Updated July 2026 · 7 min read

CELPIP Speaking is short on paper — 15 to 20 minutes total — but it counts equally toward your final CLB profile alongside Listening, Reading, and Writing. Unlike IELTS, there's no examiner in the room: you speak into a microphone, a timer counts down, and your response is recorded and scored afterward.

Quick answer: 8 tasks, no live examiner, each with its own short prep time (roughly 30-90 seconds) followed by a response window (roughly 60-90 seconds). Scored on coherence and meaning, vocabulary, listenability (pronunciation, intonation, fluency), and task fulfillment.

Why the "no examiner" format changes your strategy

Many test-takers find recording their answer less intimidating than a live interview — no eye contact pressure, no reading a stranger's reactions. But it introduces a different challenge: there's no one to redirect you if you go off-topic, and the recording captures every pause, restart, and "um" exactly as it happened. Structure matters more here, not less.

The 8 tasks, at a glance

TaskWhat it asks for
1. Giving AdviceHelp someone make a decision or prepare for something
2. Personal ExperienceDescribe something from your own life related to a prompt
3. Describing a SceneDescribe what's happening in an image
4. Making PredictionsPredict what happens next in a scenario
5. Comparing and PersuadingCompare two options and argue for one
6. Dealing with a Difficult SituationRespond to an awkward or challenging scenario
7. Expressing OpinionsGive and support your view on an issue
8. Describing an Unusual SituationTalk through something out of the ordinary

A structure that works across all 8 tasks

Rather than improvising a different approach for each task, use one consistent shape every time: open with your main point, develop it with a reason and a specific example, then close with a brief wrap-up. This structure is easy to fall back on under time pressure, and it directly serves the "coherence and meaning" scoring criterion — raters are explicitly checking whether your ideas are logically organized, not just whether your grammar is clean.

What the four scoring criteria actually mean

  • Content/coherence: does your answer have a clear structure, and does it develop one idea properly rather than jumping between several half-finished thoughts?
  • Vocabulary: range and precision — using a more specific word instead of a generic one, where it fits naturally.
  • Listenability: pronunciation, intonation, and fluency. This isn't about accent — it's about whether a listener can follow you comfortably.
  • Task fulfillment: did you actually answer what was asked? A well-spoken response that drifts off-topic still loses marks here.

Practical tips that matter most

  • Use your full prep time. Even 30 seconds is enough to plan a rough opening line and your main example — don't start speaking immediately out of nervousness.
  • Fill your response window. Stopping well before time runs out generally signals underdeveloped content.
  • Practice by recording yourself. This is the one CELPIP skill that's hard to improve just by reading about it — you need to hear your own pacing and hesitations to fix them.
  • Don't over-focus on accent. Clarity and natural pacing matter far more than sounding a particular way.
