What Happens If You Fail the Canadian Citizenship Test?

Updated July 2026 · 5 min read

If you didn't pass, take a breath — a meaningful share of applicants don't pass on the first attempt, and it does not put your application at risk on its own. Here's exactly what happens next, step by step.

The short version: a second attempt is generally scheduled automatically, at no extra cost, usually within a few weeks. Your application stays open the whole time. A second miss typically leads to an interview, not an automatic refusal.

The general process, step by step

1
You get your result. For online or in-person tests, results are often available the same day.
2
A second attempt is scheduled. This typically happens automatically, without you needing to contact IRCC or resubmit anything. You'll receive a notice with your new date.
3
You retake the test. Same format — 20 questions, 75% to pass — but different specific questions.
4
If you pass, you move forward toward the citizenship ceremony like any other applicant.
5
If you don't pass a second time, the process generally moves to an interview with a citizenship official, who can assess your knowledge directly rather than through a written test.

Does failing affect your permanent resident status?

No. Failing the knowledge test doesn't change your status as a permanent resident, and it isn't a strike against your file. It simply means another step in the same process.

What to actually do differently before the retake

Instead of re-reading the whole guide the same way, it usually helps more to identify which specific areas tripped you up and drill those with timed practice tests rather than passive reading. Mixing question formats (multiple choice, true/false) and reviewing explanations for wrong answers — not just the right answer — tends to stick better than rereading long passages.
